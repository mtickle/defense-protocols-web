title: "My Testimony"
---


When we think of a testimony, we often think of three parts: life before Jesus, how you met Jesus, and life after. For me, it wasn't one single event, but a story of God patiently calling me back, again and again.

My story with faith started young. I was baptized as a Lutheran in my elementary school gymnasium in Rapid City, SD, part of a new church that literally grew from a pastor’s basement into a full-fledged building. I saw God move, but as a kid, I didn't fully grasp it.

After high school, I joined the Army and life got complicated. A young marriage failed, my military career ended unexpectedly, and my faith was damaged. I entered a dark period—a cycle of bad decisions where I felt stuck, afraid of being alone, and honestly, I found a strange comfort in my own sadness. I was ignoring God's call.

Then came March of 2008. God found a way to intersect the lives of two people who truly needed each other and didn't know it. All of which happened in the shadow of a bitter social media post by a third party who thought she could cause harm. Needless to say, her plan backfired.

Meeting Alison was like driving through one of those long, dark mountain tunnels... and suddenly breaking out into the bright sunlight. You have to squint for a moment, but then you realize—you're out of the darkness. God took me by the hand and walked me out of my sin and sadness and into a new life with this amazing woman who would become my wife and mother of our children. 

That was my biggest turning point. And the chapters just keep getting better. We have three beautiful and unique children. I’ve had the joy of seeing Alison get baptized right here in this church. I’ve been blessed to serve with our greeting team, to learn to trust God on the praise team, and to work with our youth. Now, I have the honor of becoming a deacon.

Life still isn't perfect. We have struggles like anyone else. But the difference now is this: when I feel like a failure, when I'm afraid or overwhelmed, I don't have to stay in that darkness. I take it all to the throne room. I can fall on my knees and take it all to Jesus. And He takes my fears, He takes my burdens, and He turns on the light a little brighter.

All the goodness in my life is because of His moment-by-moment salvation.
