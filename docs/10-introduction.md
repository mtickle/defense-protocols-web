In September 2025, I was ordained as a deacon in my church.  It was an emotional service; I stood before the congregation, gave my testimony (as you read in the previous section), and felt the weight of hands laid upon me in prayer. I was a musician, a youth leader, and now a deacon. I felt like my walk with God was about to reach new heights. 

It was only a matter of days before the bottom fell out. 

It started with normal life problems—thousands of dollars in car repairs, a flat tire in a distant city, financial strain, and family tension. But it quickly morphed into something darker: raw, intense anxiety. I felt physically sick, seized by hot flashes of panic and an urge to run for the hills. The anxiety became so blinding and burning that I couldn't see a way out. It was literally taking the breath out of me. I eventually found myself planning my own death. I wrote a note to my family—a note my wife eventually found—and I stood on the precipice of total collapse. 

## The Library Moment 

Several weeks later, I was sitting in a public library, staring at a laptop, pretending to work but actually paralyzed by fear. There, in the seemingly impossible library silence, the Holy Spirit broke through the noise. He impressed a simple, undeniable conviction on my heart: **You do not have to go through this alone, and neither should others.** 

I realized two things in that moment: 

* I had the ultimate toolkit right in front of me: The Sovereign Word of God.
* I was under a relentless attack by the enemy. 

The Bible tells us that when we draw closer to God, the enemy will do anything to stop us. I had just committed my life to service, and the enemy was furious. I had been spiritually lax, leaving weaknesses in my spiritual barrier exposed, and he freely began his attack. 

## Building the Defense Protocols 

I needed a weapon.  I didn't need a heavy theological textbook I had to read for hours; I needed a flashcard, something I could pull up on my phone in a moment of doubt (or any other situation) that would tell me exactly what to do. 

So, I got to work. Using the Bible as my foundation—and yes, utilizing modern AI tools to help me organize and research theological concepts—I built these Biblical defense protocols, and now, in the hope of it helping more than just me, I am sharing them with you. 

I wanted a system that was portable, easy and quick to digest, sharable and yes, simple. All steps of each protocol are written as: 

* **A Verse:** The weapon from Scripture. 
* **Insight:** A quick summary of how and what I could be feeling. 
* **The Trigger:** How to identify the attack. 
* **The Action:** What to do physically and spiritually. 
* **The Goal:** The specific outcome I needed to reach. 

## How to Use This Guide 

This book is the result of that work. It is a collection of the protocols I built to save my own life and sanity. We will cover several different defense protocols, ranging from anxiety to sudden storms to depression, and finally, to seal our rejection of the enemy among us, spiritual overflow—learning to live so full of God that the enemy cannot find a foothold. 

I am neither a theologian nor counsellor. I am merely a believer in my Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, and a believer in the power of prayer. My theology game is admittedly weak at times. While I used tools and the advice of friends to help me build this, every word has been prayed over and vetted against Scripture. 

This is not an instant cure. Consider it a stepping stone. It is a field manual for when you feel the fire burning and the enemy reaching out to touch you.

**Administrative notes:** 

All verses are from the New International Version (NIV) and are presented in a format recommended by both the AP Style Guide, the Chicago Manual of Style, and ChristianWriters.com. 

The "enemy" here is always the devil or Satan, unless it is specifically part of a Bible verse. I will always refer to it as the enemy in lower case. It does not deserve the recognition of a capitalized proper noun.
